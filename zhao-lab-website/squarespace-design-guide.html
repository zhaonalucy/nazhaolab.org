Research
The Questions That Drive Us

Cancer cells don't just carry mutations — they change the rules of how messages become proteins. This process, called translational regulation, is one of the most underexplored layers of cancer biology. We currently focus on breast cancer, with particular emphasis on triple-negative breast cancer (TNBC), one of the most aggressive subtypes with the fewest options.

Question I

How does cancer rewire its protein-making machinery?

Cancer cells co-opt translational regulators — like eIF4A, eIF4E, and eIF4G2 — to selectively amplify translation of specific oncogenic messages. We map these dependencies to find molecular switches cancer flips.

Polysome profiling Ribosome footprinting Functional genomics
Question II

How does the tumor ecosystem reshape translation?

Tumors are complex ecosystems. We investigate how immune cells, stromal signals, and stress reprogram the translational landscape, creating feedback loops that fuel metastasis and drug resistance.

Single-cell analysis Spatial transcriptomics Organoid models
Question III

Can we target translation to treat patients?

This isn't purely academic — our discovery that eIF4A drives metastasis has already contributed to a clinical trial. We continue developing strategies to therapeutically target translational dependencies.

Preclinical models Drug development Clinical collaboration
🎯

From Discovery to Clinical Trial

Our research on eIF4A's role in metastasis directly contributed to a Phase 1/2 clinical trial (NCT03950570) — demonstrating that fundamental insights into translational regulation can make a real difference for cancer patients.

Join Us
Work With Us

We're looking for curious, motivated scientists who want their work to matter.

The Zhao Lab is a collaborative environment where trainees develop real expertise in cancer biology, RNA regulation, computational genomics, and translational research. We value scientific rigor, creativity, and the drive to make discoveries that change how we treat cancer.

We're a young, growing lab — every member shapes our direction. If you're excited about fundamental biology with therapeutic impact, we'd love to hear from you.

We are currently seeking:
  • Postdoctoral fellows with expertise in cancer biology, RNA biology, or computational genomics
  • Graduate students through BCM's MCB, Cancer Biology, or related programs
  • Motivated undergraduate researchers for summer or year-round projects

3. In Design → Site Styles: set primary color to #0891b2, button color to #0891b2

8. Add navigation links in Pages → Main Navigation
